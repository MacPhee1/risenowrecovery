01
The Foundation

What This Space Is Built For

The Risenow community exists for one purpose — to support men who have decided enough is enough. Every feature, every rule, and every decision about how this space operates comes back to that purpose.

Men come here at difficult moments. Some are at the beginning. Some are returning after a setback. Some are months in and still finding it hard. The common thread is the decision to change — and the need for a space where that decision is met with honesty rather than judgment.

"You do not need to have it figured out to be here. You need to have made the decision. This community is for what comes after that."

The anonymity is intentional. You are a username. Nothing else identifies you here. That anonymity is what makes the honesty possible — and the honesty is what makes the community worth being part of.

02
The Rules

What We Expect From Every Member

These are not suggestions. They are the terms on which this community operates. Every member is held to the same standard regardless of how long they have been here.

    ✓
    Be honest
    Share what is actually happening — not the version you think people want to hear. A hard day posted honestly helps more men than a highlight reel. The community is built on truthful accounts, setbacks included.
    ✓
    Protect the anonymity of others
    What is shared in this community stays in this community. Do not screenshot, share, or reference another member's posts outside this space. Every man here has trusted this community with something personal. Respect that trust.
    ✓
    Respond with respect
    When someone shares a difficult day, respond the way you would want someone to respond to you. No lectures. No shame. No "you should have known better." Support first, always.
    ✓
    Stay on topic
    This community is specifically about porn recovery, PIED, and the habits and relationships affected by long-term use. Keep posts relevant. There are better places for everything else.
    ✓
    Come back after a setback
    A setback is not the end of your membership here. Post it, reset the counter, and keep going. The men who reach 90 days almost universally have resets in their history. Returning is the move.
03
Boundaries

What Is Not Allowed Here

The following behaviours are incompatible with this community. They will be addressed immediately and will result in removal where necessary.

    ✗
    Shaming or judging other members
    There is no place here for making another man feel worse about where he is. Every person in this community is working on something difficult. That deserves respect, not criticism.
    ✗
    Sharing explicit or triggering content
    This is a recovery community. Posting or linking to pornographic, sexually explicit, or triggering content of any kind is strictly prohibited — including descriptions, links, images, and references to specific content.
    ✗
    Unsolicited advice or prescriptive responses
    Unless someone has asked for your input, do not prescribe what they should be doing. Sharing your own experience is welcome. Telling someone what their experience means or what they need to do is not.
    ✗
    Promotion, spam, or self-promotion
    No affiliate links, no product promotion, no soliciting clients or followers, no spam of any kind. If you have a resource you genuinely believe helps, contact us directly.
    ✗
    Attempting to identify other members
    Do not attempt to connect a username to a real identity. Do not ask for personal information. Any attempt to undermine the anonymity of any member will result in immediate removal.
    ✗
    Harassment of any kind
    Any form of harassment toward any member will result in immediate and permanent removal with no opportunity for appeal.

05
Important

A Note on Mental Health and Crisis Support

This community is a peer support space, not a substitute for professional mental health care. The men here can offer genuine understanding and solidarity — but they are not therapists, counsellors, or crisis workers.

If you are experiencing thoughts of self-harm or suicide, please reach out to a professional service immediately. findahelpline.com lists crisis lines for every country.

If you see a post that suggests someone may be in crisis, do not ignore it. Use the report function immediately. Responding with care while also flagging it is the right move.

Recovery from long-term pornography use can surface difficult emotions — anxiety, depression, shame, grief. These are real and they deserve professional attention alongside community support.

06
The Spirit of This Space

What We Are Building Here

Rules describe the floor. This section describes the ceiling — what this community looks like when it is working as intended.

A man posts on Day 1 and someone on Day 60 responds with exactly what he needed to hear — not a lecture, not a list of tips, just honest acknowledgement that Day 1 is the hardest and that the community is here for Day 2 as well.

A man has a setback and posts it honestly. The responses do not pile on. They recognise the act of returning as the thing that matters most — and they say so.

A man reaches 90 days. The community marks it the way it deserves — not with hollow congratulations but with the specific recognition that 90 days of this particular fight is genuinely hard-won.

That is the community we are building. These guidelines exist to protect it.

"Built for men who decided enough is enough. The community is what comes after the decision."
